# HR Analytics Dashboard — Employee Attrition Intelligence

## 📌 Project Overview
Analysed 1,470 employee records to identify why employees 
are leaving, which departments are most at risk, and how 
much attrition is costing the business.

## 🎯 Business Problem
The company's attrition rate of 17.4% is significantly above 
the industry benchmark of 10-12%. HR needs to understand the 
root causes to take targeted retention action.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
- **SQL (MySQL)** — Data extraction and analysis
- **Excel** — Pivot tables and cost modelling
- **Power BI** — Interactive dashboard

## 📊 Key Business Insights
- 📍 Overall attrition rate: **17.4%** (256 out of 1,470 employees)
- 💰 Total attrition cost: **₹12.9M** across all departments
- 🏢 R&D department highest cost centre: **₹7.6M**
- ⏰ **63% of attrition happens within first 2 years** of joining
- 👤 Peak exit age group: **28–35 years** (career growth phase)
- 💡 Potential saving if attrition reduced to 12%: **~₹4M annually**

## 🔍 SQL Analysis Performed
- Overall attrition rate calculation
- Attrition by department and salary band
- Average tenure of employees who left vs stayed
- Top job roles with highest attrition
- Window functions: RANK() by attrition count
- Attrition cost calculation per department

## 💼 Business Recommendation
Focus retention efforts on R&D early-tenure employees 
(0-2 years) with structured growth plans and 6-month 
check-ins — projected to recover ₹4M in annual 
attrition costs.
